## Understanding Indian Sargam Notation

Indian music uses **Sargam** — a system of seven notes similar to Western Do-Re-Mi. This songbook uses a **word-based notation** system for easy reading.

---

### The Seven Natural Notes (Shuddh Swar)

| Sargam | Written As | Western Equivalent | Solfege |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| सा (Sa) | **Sa** | C | DO |
| रे (Re) | **Re** | D | RE |
| ग (Ga) | **Ga** | E | MI |
| म (ma) | **ma** | F | FA |
| प (Pa) | **Pa** | G | SO |
| ध (Dha) | **Dha** | A | LA |
| नि (Ni) | **Ni** | B | TI |

> **Note:** Shuddh Ma is written in **lowercase** as **ma** to distinguish it from Tivra Ma (**Ma**).

---

### Komal Swar (Flat Notes)

Four notes can be flattened (lowered by a half-step). They are marked with **(k)**:

| Note | Written As | Western Equivalent |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Komal Re | **Re(k)** | Db |
| Komal Ga | **Ga(k)** | Eb |
| Komal Dha | **Dha(k)** | Ab |
| Komal Ni | **Ni(k)** | Bb |

---

### Tivra Swar (Sharp Note)

Only Ma can be sharpened (raised by a half-step):

| Note | Written As | Western Equivalent |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Shuddh Ma | **ma** | F |
| Tivra Ma | **Ma** | F# |

> **Tip:** Lowercase **ma** = natural (Shuddh). Uppercase **Ma** = sharp (Tivra).

---

### Octaves

Notes can be played in three octave ranges:

#### Middle Octave (Madhya Saptak) — Default
Written normally: **Sa Re Ga ma Pa Dha Ni**

#### Low Octave (Mandra Saptak)
Written in **lowercase**: **pa dha ni**

These are the notes below the main octave. Example:
- **pa** = low Pa (G below middle C)
- **dha** = low Dha (A below middle C)
- **ni** = low Ni (B below middle C)

#### High Octave (Taar Saptak)
Written with a **quote mark** (apostrophe) suffix:

| Note | Written As |
|------|-----------|
| High Sa | **Sa'** |
| High Re | **Re'** |
| High Ga | **Ga'** |
| High Ma (Shuddh) | **ma'** |
| High Ma (Tivra) | **Ma'** |
| High Pa | **Pa'** |

---

### Special Symbols

| Symbol | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **—** | Sustain / Hold the note longer | Sa— (hold Sa) |
| **~** | Glide smoothly between notes | Ga~Re (slide from Ga to Re) |
| **\|** | Bar line / phrase separator | Sa Re \| Ga ma |
| **(  )** | Grace note (quick ornamental touch) | (ni)Sa = quick ni before Sa |
| **:** | Hold / extend the note | Sa: (sustained Sa) |

---

### Reading a Song Line

Each song line has two or three parts:

```
Lyrics:    Lag ja gale
Indian:    Re— Ga~Ga Re Ga—
Western:   D— E~E D E—
```

- **Lyrics** — the words to sing
- **Indian** — Sargam notation (what notes to play)
- **Western** — Same notes in Western letter names (C D E F G A B)

Read left to right. Each sargam note aligns roughly with the syllable above it.

### Tips for Beginners

1. **Start slow** — play each note separately, then gradually speed up
2. **Use the toggle** — switch between Indian / Western / Both views using the buttons at the top of each song
3. **Listen first** — find the song on YouTube and listen while following the notation
4. **Low octave notes** (lowercase: pa, dha, ni) sound deeper — move left on a keyboard
5. **High octave notes** (with quote: Sa', Re') sound higher — move right on a keyboard
6. **Grace notes in parentheses** are quick touches — don't linger on them
