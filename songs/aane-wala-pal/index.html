About

This iconic philosophical ballad captures the fleeting nature of time and love with profound simplicity. Composed by R.D. Burman in the melancholic yet serene Raag Kirwani, Kishore Kumar's soulful rendition is beautifully complemented by Gulzar's poetic genius and a minimalist acoustic arrangement.

  • 1 Kishore Kumar won the Filmfare Award for Best Male Playback Singer for this song, while Gulzar won the Filmfare Award for Best Lyricist.
  • 2 The song features a remarkably minimalist arrangement dominated by an acoustic guitar and a haunting flute, showcasing R.D. Burman's versatility beyond his signature upbeat, brass-heavy compositions.
  • 3 Raag Kirwani, the classical foundation of this song, is a Carnatic import into Hindustani music that resembles the Western harmonic minor scale, making it perfect for evoking deep nostalgia and bittersweet emotions.
  • 4 The opening acoustic guitar arpeggio is one of the most recognized intros in Indian cinema history, instantly setting the song's contemplative and reflective mood.
