About

This iconic romantic ballad from 'Criminal' is a testament to M. M. Keeravani's melodic genius, beautifully rendered by Kumar Sanu and Alka Yagnik. Its soulful melody, deeply rooted in Indian classical sensibilities, creates an atmosphere of tender longing and profound love. The song's enduring appeal lies in its elegant composition and the heartfelt emotion it conveys, making it a timeless classic of 90s Bollywood.

  • 1 The music director, M. M. Keeravani, is also known as M. M. Kreem in Hindi cinema and later gained international acclaim, winning an Oscar for 'Naatu Naatu' from 'RRR'.
  • 2 The film 'Criminal' was a bilingual production, released in both Hindi and Telugu (as 'The Villain'), with Keeravani composing for both versions.
  • 3 Besides the popular duet by Kumar Sanu and Alka Yagnik, there is also a beloved female solo version sung by K. S. Chithra, showcasing the song's versatility.
  • 4 The song's melody is strongly based on Raag Yaman, a prominent evening raga known for its romantic and devotional mood, characterized by the use of teevra Madhyam.
