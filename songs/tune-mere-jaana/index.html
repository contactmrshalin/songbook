How to Read Notation

The Seven Natural Notes (Shuddh Swar)

SargamWritten AsWesternSolfege
सा (Sa)SaCDO
रे (Re)ReDRE
ग (Ga)GaEMI
म (ma)maFFA
प (Pa)PaGSO
ध (Dha)DhaALA
नि (Ni)NiBTI

Shuddh Ma is written in lowercase as ma to distinguish it from Tivra Ma (Ma).

Komal Swar (Flat Notes)

Four notes can be flattened. They are marked with (k):

NoteWritten AsWestern
Komal ReRe(k)Db
Komal GaGa(k)Eb
Komal DhaDha(k)Ab
Komal NiNi(k)Bb

Tivra Swar (Sharp Note)

NoteWritten AsWestern
Shuddh MamaF
Tivra MaMaF#

Lowercase ma = natural. Uppercase Ma = sharp.

Octaves

Middle Octave (default): Sa Re Ga ma Pa Dha Ni

Low Octave (lowercase): pa dha ni

High Octave (quote suffix): Sa' Re' Ga' ma' Ma' Pa'

Special Symbols

SymbolMeaningExample
—Sustain / HoldSa— (hold Sa)
~Glide between notesGa~Re (slide)
|Bar line / separatorSa Re | Ga ma
( )Grace note(ni)Sa = quick ni before Sa
:Hold / extendSa: (sustained)

Reading a Song Line

Lyrics:    Lag ja gale
Indian:    Re— Ga~Ga Re Ga—
Western:   D— E~E D E—

Read left to right. Each sargam note aligns with the syllable above it.
