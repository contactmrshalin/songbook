About

Achyutam Keshavam is a timeless Hindu devotional bhajan, a heartfelt ode to Lord Krishna, one of the most revered deities in Hinduism. Its simple yet profound melody and lyrics evoke a sense of peace and devotion, making it a staple in temples, homes, and spiritual gatherings across India. The chant-like quality of the composition encourages meditative reflection on the divine attributes of Krishna.

  • 1 The lyrics are part of the 'Madhurashtakam' (अधरं मधुरं वदनं मधुरं...), a beautiful Sanskrit devotional poem composed by the 15th-century philosopher and saint, Vallabhacharya, praising the sweetness of Lord Krishna.
  • 2 The bhajan enumerates various names and epithets of Lord Krishna (Vishnu), such as Achyuta (the infallible), Keshava (one with beautiful hair, or killer of the demon Keshi), Rama, Narayana, Damodara, and Vasudeva, each carrying deep spiritual significance.
  • 3 Despite its ancient origins, 'Achyutam Keshavam' gained immense popularity in contemporary times through numerous renditions by various devotional singers, becoming a widely recognized and cherished bhajan.
  • 4 The simplicity of its melodic structure makes it accessible for communal singing and meditation, often serving as an entry point for many into the world of Hindu devotional music.
